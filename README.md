# Wide FoV VR Research

Independent research and development into custom wide field-of-view (FoV) VR headset architectures.

---

## Overview

This project explores experimental approaches to building VR headsets with significantly expanded horizontal field of view.

The focus is on integrating multiple displays and custom optical configurations to investigate scalable solutions for ultra-wide FoV systems.

This work combines mechanical design, optics, and display pipeline considerations to better understand the challenges involved in next-generation VR hardware.

---

## Objectives

- Explore multi-display configurations for increased FoV  
- Investigate optical layouts for wide-angle image projection  
- Develop mounting and alignment strategies for displays and lenses  
- Evaluate distortion and calibration requirements  
- Understand hardware constraints (bandwidth, drivers, synchronization)  

---

## Current Architecture Under Study

- Multi-display per-eye configurations  
- Custom lens positioning and alignment  
- Angled or segmented display layouts  
- Independent display control and calibration  

---

## Hardware Being Evaluated

- 1440x1440 @ 120Hz LCD panels (WiseCoco)  
- Binocular display driver boards  
- DisplayPort input systems  
- Custom mounting structures (3D printed)  
- VR tracking integration (SteamVR ecosystem)  

---

## Key Challenges

### Optical Alignment
Ensuring consistent image projection across multiple display regions.

### Distortion Correction
Each display requires independent distortion handling depending on lens geometry and placement.

### Seam Visibility
Managing transitions between display regions to maintain a continuous visual experience.

### Display Synchronization
Ensuring consistent timing and output across multiple panels.

### Mechanical Constraints
Designing a compact, adjustable structure to support accurate positioning of all components.

---

## Current Status

- Initial hardware acquired and tested  
- Display driver functionality validated  
- Early-stage mounting and layout concepts in development  
- Ongoing investigation into optical alignment and calibration  

---

## Next Steps

- Finalize mechanical layout for multi-display configuration  
- Develop adjustable mounting system for alignment testing  
- Begin distortion calibration experiments  
- Explore software-side rendering considerations  
- Evaluate seam behavior under different configurations
